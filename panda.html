PANDA

Giant pandas (Ailuropoda melanoleuca) are beloved bears native to China, known for their black-and-white fur. They typically weigh 70 to 120 kg (154 to 264 lb). Despite being carnivores by classification, pandas have a diet that is 99% bamboo.

Pandas are solitary animals, spending most of their time eating or resting. They have a pseudo-thumb, an elongated wrist bone, which helps them grasp bamboo. Pandas are endangered, with conservation efforts ongoing to protect them.

They live in temperate broadleaf and mixed forests with dense bamboo undergrowth, typically at elevations between 1,200 to 3,400 m (4,000 to 11,000 ft).
