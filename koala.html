KOALA

Koalas (Phascolarctos cinereus) are marsupials native to Australia, often mistaken for bears. They weigh 4 to 15 kg (9 to 33 lb) and have thick gray fur with large, fluffy ears. Koalas spend most of their lives in eucalyptus trees.

They are strict herbivores, feeding exclusively on eucalyptus leaves, which are low in nutrients, so they sleep up to 20 hours a day to conserve energy. Koalas are solitary but may share overlapping territories.

Koalas are found in eucalyptus forests and woodlands along the eastern and southeastern coasts of Australia.
