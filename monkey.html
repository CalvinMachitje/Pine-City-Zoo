MONKEY

The dusky leaf monkey (Trachypithecus obscurus), also known as the spectacled langur, is a primate species found in Southeast Asia, including Malaysia, Thailand, and Myanmar. They are characterized by their dark gray fur, lighter underparts, and distinctive white rings around their eyes, giving them a spectacled appearance. Adults typically weigh 6 to 9 kg (13 to 20 lb).

These monkeys are arboreal, spending most of their time in trees. They are folivores, with a diet mainly consisting of leaves, though they also eat fruits and flowers. Dusky leaf monkeys live in small groups led by a dominant male and are known for their loud vocalizations.

They prefer dense rainforests and forested hills, where they can find ample foliage and protection from predators like leopards and eagles.
