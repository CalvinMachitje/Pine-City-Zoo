RHINO-BEETLE

Rhinoceros beetles (family Scarabaeidae, subfamily Dynastinae) are large insects known for the horn-like structures on their heads, resembling a rhinoceros. They can grow up to 6 cm (2.4 in) long and are among the strongest animals relative to their size, capable of lifting 850 times their own weight.

These beetles are primarily nocturnal and feed on decaying wood, sap, and fruit. Males use their horns to fight over mates. Despite their intimidating appearance, they are harmless to humans.

Rhinoceros beetles are found in tropical and subtropical forests, often in areas with plenty of decaying logs and vegetation.
