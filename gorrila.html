GORILLA

Gorillas (genus Gorilla) are the largest primates, native to the forests of central Sub-Saharan Africa. Males, known as silverbacks, can weigh up to 180 kg (400 lb) and stand up to 1.8 m (5.9 ft) tall. Gorillas have robust builds and dark fur.

They live in family groups led by a dominant silverback and are primarily herbivorous, eating leaves, stems, and fruits. Gorillas are known for their social behavior and use of tools in the wild.

Gorillas inhabit dense rainforests and mountainous regions, often at elevations up to 4,000 m (13,000 ft).
